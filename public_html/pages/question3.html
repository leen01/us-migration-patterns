The Rust Belt States

The rust-belt states are those found near the great lakes. The states that generally considered rust-belt states are in the Midwest of the United States (Indiana, Illinois, Michigan, Missouri, Ohio, Wisconsin) along with Pennsylvania, West Virginia, and part of New York. The region's prosperity sprang from the abundance of natural resources, including coal, iron ore reserves, rivers and lakes for transportation. Between 1950s to 1980s the region experienced a significant economic decline an dislocation because of competition, increased labour costs, and failure to modernize equipment and infrastructure, and technological advancements that replaced workers.
